# Founder's reasoning · 1989 textile exit

**Captured from:** Don Tomás Mendoza, recalling Lolo Salvador's framing
**Sessions:** Three working sessions, 2026-04-15 / 2026-04-29 / 2026-05-09
**Concierge present:** Maria Rivera (lead), Rafael Velasco (counsel context)

## What this page is

The full preserved reasoning behind the family's 1989 decision to exit textile manufacturing. The decision itself is documented at [[exit-textiles-1989]]; this page is the deeper reasoning, captured from Don Tomás verbatim where possible, paraphrased where his words trailed.

## The framing Lolo Salvador returned to

> "Three generations of cotton. Three generations is enough."

He said this — in some form — to Don Tomás at least a dozen times across 1987 and 1988, before any of the actual decision-making started. Don Tomás recalls it as the framing under which all the analysis was done. The numerical case mattered. The framing was decisive.

## What the analysis showed

Lolo Salvador commissioned a quiet 1987 assessment from a Hong Kong consultancy he had used in the early 1980s. The brief, reconstructed from Don Tomás's recollection:

- Cost-per-yard advantage versus emerging Korean and Taiwanese mills: closing at ~3% per year.
- Capital required for modernization to close the gap: roughly twice the family's then-annual free cash flow.
- Realistic horizon at which textile would remain capital-positive: 6–9 years.
- Realistic horizon at which the exit would still command a premium: 3–5 years.

The conclusion was that the family had a 2–4 year window where exiting capital-positive was still possible. The analysis was filed in Lolo Salvador's personal study and is one of the documents Atrium is in the process of locating in the family archive.

## What Lolo Salvador said to Don Tomás before the family meeting

This is the line Don Tomás returns to most often:

> "You are going to run this family for forty years. Decide what you want to be defending in 1995, in 2005, in 2015. Not what your father has been defending."

## What this preserved reasoning does for the family

Every "should we exit X?" or "should we double down on Y?" question the council now faces is implicitly asked against this precedent. Until this page existed, the precedent lived in Don Tomás's head. Now it is on the wiki and can be read by Isabel and Marco directly.

Isabel will read this page before the [[next-gen-council-agenda]] briefing on 2026-05-22. It is the first time a generation-3 family member reads Lolo Salvador's reasoning in full.
