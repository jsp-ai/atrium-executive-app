# Founder's reasoning · Singapore over Hong Kong

**Captured from:** Don Tomás Mendoza
**Sessions:** Two working sessions, 2026-05-02 and 2026-05-14
**Concierge:** Maria Rivera

## Why this matters

The family chose Singapore over Hong Kong as the locus of the next generation's operating life. This is a decision Don Tomás has held quietly since around 2008. Until now it has not been documented; Isabel and Marco have grown up around the choice without ever having heard Don Tomás explain it.

The [[singapore-sfo-licensing]] engagement made the documentation urgent.

## Don Tomás's framing

> "We are a Filipino family. The seat is Manila. The seat does not move."

> "Hong Kong is the obvious answer for a family with our shape. It is the wrong answer for us specifically."

The reasoning that follows:

1. **Hong Kong's center of gravity is China.** The Mendoza family's businesses are not Chinese, the family's relationships are not Chinese, and the next generation's operating muscle is not built on Chinese counterparty understanding. Putting the family-office shell in Hong Kong would put it in a city the next generation does not yet read.

2. **Singapore is a market the family already speaks.** The Watanabe relationship transits Singapore. The 1991 trust amendments were drafted with Singapore counsel review. Isabel's residence is Singapore. The relationships are real.

3. **Singapore's regulatory frame for family offices is mature.** Hong Kong's framework is younger, less stable, and more politically sensitive in 2026 than at any point in the last twenty years.

4. **Singapore is closer to the Philippines.** Don Tomás returns to this last because it is the least intellectually interesting but the most operationally true: Manila to Singapore is 3.5 hours. Manila to Hong Kong is 2 hours, but the operating life inside Hong Kong is harder.

## What we are not saying

This reasoning is about where the family's investment shell lives. It is not a statement about the family's operating businesses, which remain Philippines-domiciled, nor about the family's seat, which remains Manila.
