# Singapore SFO licensing

**Status:** Active · third counsel revision in flight
**Lead counsel:** Drew & Napier (Singapore)
**Atrium concierge:** Rafael Velasco
**Family lead:** [[isabel-mendoza-reyes]] (shadowing)

## What this is

The Mendoza family is in the process of obtaining a single-family-office license under the Singapore Monetary Authority's Variable Capital Company framework. This is the legal scaffolding for the next phase of the family's wealth management — anchored in Singapore, where Isabel's residence is — without disturbing the Philippines operating presence.

## Where we are this week

Third counsel revision is with Drew & Napier. The contested point is the trustee-class language: the 2024 amendments to the [[mendoza-family-trust]] used class language that the Singapore regulator's standard form does not recognize, and counsel's first two revisions tried to concede that point. Don Tomás's instruction (2026-05-18) was to defend the original trustee-class framing rather than concede it.

Rafael is back to counsel with that instruction; the revised draft is expected Thursday.

## Why the family is doing this

Three reasons preserved across the working sessions with Don Tomás and Isabel:

1. **Isabel's center of gravity is Singapore.** The SFO follows the operating life of the next generation, not the seat of the trust.
2. **Singapore's regulatory frame is the most mature for family-office structuring in the region.** The Hong Kong alternative was considered and rejected; see the working notes filed at this engagement.
3. **The Philippines presence does not move.** Mendoza Holdings stays Philippines-domiciled. The trust stays Philippines-governed. The SFO sits above the family's investment activity, not above the operating businesses.

## What is not in scope

This engagement does not touch the trust structure itself, the operating units under [[mendoza-holdings]], or the foundation. Those are governed elsewhere in this wiki.
