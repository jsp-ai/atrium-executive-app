# Decline Tokyo logistics co-investment

**Decision-maker:** Family council
**Date resolved:** 2026-05-15
**Vote:** Unanimous to decline
**Decision:** Decline participation in the proposed Tokyo logistics co-investment with the Watanabe family.

## The question

The Watanabe family (Tokyo, third generation, ~$2B AUM) approached the Mendoza family in February 2026 with a co-investment vehicle into a Tokyo-area logistics consolidation. Proposed entry: $40M for ~12% of the co-invest vehicle.

## The reasoning the council preserved

Don Tomás opened the discussion with a reference to [[exit-textiles-1989]]: *"We have always understood when to enter a market. We have understood less often when not to enter one."*

Three reasons emerged:

1. **No operating presence in Tokyo.** Co-investment without an operating chair turns the family into a passive minority. The family has not historically been a passive minority and has nothing to learn from being one.
2. **The Watanabe-Mendoza relationship is asymmetric in our favor right now.** Co-investing collapses that asymmetry into a transactional one. The relationship is worth more than the deal.
3. **Capital is not the constraint.** The family does not currently need to find a return for $40M; the trust has its own deployment cadence. Adding a $40M Tokyo logistics position does not advance any current family goal.

## What happens next

- The relationship with the Watanabe family is preserved, with Doña Carmen reaching out for an in-person visit in Q4 2026.
- The decision page is the citation for any future "should we co-invest with X" question.
