# The 1989 textile exit

**Decision-maker:** Lolo Salvador Mendoza (with Don Tomás as second voice)
**Date executed:** September 1989
**Decision:** Exit all textile manufacturing operations within 18 months; redeploy capital into logistics and consumer retail.

## Why this page exists

This is the single decision the family returns to most often. Every subsequent question of "should we exit X?" or "should we double down on Y?" is asked, implicitly, against the 1989 precedent. The reasoning has lived for 35 years in Don Tomás's head and in a handful of paragraphs his father dictated to him before his death.

Atrium captured the full reasoning across three working sessions with Don Tomás in April and May 2026. The working notes are filed at [[founder-1989-textile-exit]]; this page is the canonical decision page for citation purposes.

## The question, as the family remembers it

Should the Mendoza family stay in textile manufacturing — where it had been for three generations and where it had material market position — or exit while the exit was still capital-positive?

## The options considered

1. **Stay and reinvest.** Continue capital expenditure in modernization; ride the cycle.
2. **Stay and harvest.** Cease capital expenditure; run the businesses for cash for 5–7 years; exit later.
3. **Exit now.** Sell the operating units while they still commanded a premium; redeploy.

## The reasoning, in Lolo Salvador's voice

Three reasons, preserved verbatim by Don Tomás:

1. *"The cost structure does not bend. Every advantage we have is fixed by 1985 and degraded by 1995. We cannot beat the new entrants on capital cost, and we are too small to win on scale."*
2. *"The next generation does not want to inherit a fight. Tomás will not run a textile mill against the Koreans. He will run something he chose."*
3. *"What we want to keep is the family, not the trade. The family survives the trade. It will not survive a slow decline that runs ten years past obviousness."*

## What happened

Exit executed September 1989 through February 1991. Three operating units sold to three different buyers; capital redeployed into the logistics arm of [[mendoza-holdings]] and the property holdings in Cebu and Pasig. Casa Mendoza Consumer was founded in 2003 with what remained.

## Why the family still cites it

Every "should we exit" question the council faces is asked against this precedent. The most recent: 2024, against an offer for the entire holding company. See [[mendoza-holdings]] for the council's reasoning on that one.
