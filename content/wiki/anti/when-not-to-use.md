# When Atrium is not the right tool.

Atrium is built for a specific altitude and a specific kind of organization. It is the wrong tool when:

**The institutional memory still lives in two or three people's heads and the family or company is young.** At that scale the friction of maintaining a wiki exceeds the friction it removes. Come back at the moment a senior advisor announces retirement, or the moment the next generation joins the council.

**The family wants a tool it runs itself on day one.** Atrium is operated for the family, not by them. The principal reads and asks. Atrium tends the memory. If the buyer's mental model is a self-serve SaaS subscription that the family logs into and edits, the conversation is the wrong one.

**The family or company has a cultural allergy to writing things down.** Atrium is a writing-culture product. If the leadership genuinely refuses to write, the wiki will not be maintained and the value will not compound.

**The organization is being wound down or sold inside 12 months.** The compounding case requires a runway. A six-month relationship with Atrium pays for itself in a single succession event, but the moat-grade outcomes need 24+ months.

**The buyer wants a chatbot.** Atrium is not ChatGPT for executives. If the prospect's mental model is "Slack with AI" or "GPT with our data," the diagnostic is the wrong conversation and the engagement will be a disappointment.

We will tell you any of the above in writing if it applies. Saving you a quarter is worth more to us than booking the engagement.
