# What Atrium is not.

Atrium is a practice that composes institutional memory. It is not, and does not claim to be, the following:

**Not a family-office software.** Atrium does not replace the family-office accounting system, the consolidated reporting tool, the partner allocation tracker. Those systems hold rows. Atrium reads from them and composes the narrative.

**Not a wealth-management platform.** Atrium does not advise on investments, does not allocate capital, does not transact. It does not custody assets and does not generate performance reports as a primary deliverable.

**Not a fiduciary.** Atrium is not a trustee, not an executor, not a guardian, not a counsel. The family's existing fiduciaries — trustees, lawyers, accountants — remain in their roles. Atrium composes the memory their work generates.

**Not an investment-advisory product.** Atrium does not provide investment advice and does not represent itself as a regulated advisor in any jurisdiction.

**Not a chatbot.** Atrium is not "ChatGPT for principals." The conversational interface is the surface of a structured memory; it is not a generic assistant.

**Not a data lake.** Atrium does not centralize the family's operational data into a new substrate. The custodian still holds the positions. The trust administrator still holds the entity registry. Atrium reads from these.

**Not a magic.** Every claim Atrium composes carries a citation back to a wiki page, which carries a citation back to a source document. There is no implicit reasoning. There is no unsourced narrative.

If any of the above is what the family or its advisors are looking for, the diagnostic conversation will conclude that there is no fit, and we will say so in writing.
