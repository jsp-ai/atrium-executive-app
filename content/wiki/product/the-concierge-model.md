# The concierge model.

The family does not edit. The family reads and asks. Atrium operates the stack on behalf of the family.

This is the most important sentence in the practice for an audience of AI-fluent but non-technical principals. It is also the line that separates Atrium from every other tool in the executive-AI category.

A typical family-office software requires the family-office director to maintain the system: enter the holdings, reconcile the statements, tag the documents, file the receipts. The director is the operator. The principal is a downstream consumer.

Atrium inverts this. The operator is Atrium's own staff — concierges, schema lints, ingest service, monthly synthesis pass. The family principal asks questions in plain language and reads back composed answers with citations. The principal never sees a terminal, never opens GitHub Desktop, never edits a markdown file, never reconciles a feed.

If a family member wants to add to the wiki — a memo from a counsel call, a framing of a recent decision — they email or message their concierge. The concierge writes it up, sources the citations, files it under the right schema entry, commits it. The next time the principal asks a related question, the new memo is part of the answer.

This is the posture that unlocks a market the rest of executive AI has not been able to reach. The category has been built for technical operators. Atrium is built for the people the technical operators serve.

The white-glove is not an option. It is the product.
