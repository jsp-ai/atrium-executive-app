# The one-week diagnostic.

Every Atrium engagement begins with the same five business days.

**Day 1-2:** Integration map. We document the operational stack that exists today — what it is, where the data lives, who tends it, what is missing. For a family enterprise this includes the family-office system, the custodian relationships, the trust administrator, the household operations, and the counsel of record.

**Day 3:** Schema sketch. A first cut at how the family or company's operating reality wants to be modeled — entities, relationships, decisions, capital events, council resolutions. Bespoke to the situation, not generic.

**Day 4:** Three highest-leverage executive-layer interventions. Concrete: this question, on this data, for this person — the principal, an heir, or the family-office director.

**Day 5:** Full engagement proposal. Scope, timeline, pricing.

The diagnostic is fixed-fee and convertible against the full engagement. There is no commitment beyond the week.

If we conclude there is no fit, we say so in writing — including the specific reason — so the family does not spend another quarter evaluating the wrong vendor.
