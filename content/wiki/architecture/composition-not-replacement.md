# Composition, not replacement.

Atrium does not rip out your stack. It composes from it.

For a family enterprise: the custodian still holds the positions. The trust administrator still holds the entity structure. The family-office software still holds the rollups. Counsel still holds the memoranda. Household staff still hold the calendar. Atrium reads from these — through APIs, through exports, through the agent's structured ingestion — and expands the rows into narrative with citation.

There is no parallel run. There is no cutover. There is no migration. The buy decision is **additive, not substitutive.** If Atrium turns out to be wrong for the family, the operational stack is untouched and the wiki is plain markdown the family already owns. The reversal cost is the cost of archiving one folder.

This is the posture that lets Atrium be deployed in six weeks instead of six quarters. The family is not making a category-defining infrastructure decision. They are adding a thin layer above the infrastructure they already have.

The operational stack is unchanged. The work above it is what becomes legible.
