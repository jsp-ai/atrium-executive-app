# Atrium is the executive-layer operating system.

A structured wiki of your company's reasoning, relationships, decisions, and institutional memory — kept current by an AI agent, governed by a schema your company writes itself, queryable in plain language.

**It sits above your stack. It composes. It does not replace.**

Atrium is not a CRM, not an ERP, not a chat product, not a family-office software. It is the layer between the rows your operational systems hold and the narrative the principal needs to make decisions.

The product surfaces in four places:

- A **wiki** of plain-text pages with frontmatter and wikilinks, versioned in git.
- A **schema** that defines the entities the company operates on — people, holdings, decisions, capital events, products.
- A **composition engine** that reads structured outputs from the stack and expands them into narrative with citations.
- A **conversational interface** where executives, principals, and heirs ask questions in plain language and receive cited answers.
