# The family schema.

Atrium's memory layer is governed by a schema the family writes itself. For a family enterprise, the schema typically covers the following entities. Each is a wiki page type with conventions for frontmatter, required fields, and the wikilinks that bind it to the rest of the memory.

## Family Member

A natural person in the family. Generation marker. Role marker (principal · heir · advisor · in-law · spouse). Council seat. Trust beneficiary status. Links to the decisions and council resolutions they have been party to.

## Holding Entity

An operating company, an investment vehicle, a holding company, a foundation. Jurisdiction. Capital table. Officers. The reasoning for why the entity exists in the structure. Links to the decisions that shaped it.

## Trust

The legal instrument. Settlor. Trustees. Beneficiaries by class. Governing law. Distribution policy. Amendment history. Counsel of record. Each amendment is its own page, citing the resolution that authorized it.

## Decision

A consequential choice made by the principal, the council, or a trustee. The question. The options considered. The reasoning. The decision. The author. The date. The supporting documents at the time. Decisions are the most cited type of page in a mature Atrium wiki.

## Distribution

A movement of capital out of a trust or holding to a beneficiary or to philanthropy. Amount. Vehicle. Recipient. Tax treatment. The decision page that authorized it.

## Council Resolution

A formal action by the family council. Date. Members present. Motion. Vote. Resulting decisions, distributions, or appointments.

## Counsel Engagement

A law firm or counsel relationship. Scope. Lead partner. Fee structure. Matters under engagement. The memoranda they have produced (each its own page or attached document).

## Advisor Relationship

A trusted advisor — investment, tax, philanthropic, governance. Scope. Tenure. Compensation. Memos and recommendations they have produced.

## Philanthropic Commitment

A grant or pledge. Recipient. Amount. Cadence. The decision page that authorized it. The reporting cycle.

## Succession Marker

A planned or recorded handover — an appointment, a retirement, a vote of confidence, a step-down. Links the outgoing and incoming parties. The reasoning behind the timing.

---

The schema is not load-bearing. The family is. Schema entries are added and refined as the practice settles in. A schema document is itself a wiki page — `_schema.md` — versioned in the same git history as everything else.
