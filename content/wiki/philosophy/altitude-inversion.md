# The altitude inversion

**Operational AI compresses. Executive AI expands. Same model, opposite vector.**

At the operational layer the native data is unstructured — voice calls, support tickets, invoices, emails. AI's job is to compress that language into structured rows a database can hold. The friction removed is the *data-entry tax*.

At the executive layer the native data is over-structured — board packs, spreadsheets, dashboards, trust statements, custodian reports, family-office rollups. AI's job is to expand those rows back into narrative, with context, decision rationale, and qualitative texture. The friction removed is the *context-reconstruction tax*.

Deploying a compression-shaped tool at the executive layer is what generates the "feels generic" complaint. The altitude is wrong.

> Generic LLMs plateau at the C-suite because they have no institutional memory, no schema for this company, and no engine to compose decision rationale across sources. These aren't patchable bugs — they're properties of an architecture built for the wrong altitude.

The altitude inversion is the conceptual hook the entire product is built around. Every architectural choice — markdown over database, wiki over chat, citation over confidence — comes from this single observation.
