# The founder's reasoning.

The founder's reasoning is the most valuable artifact in any family enterprise — and the most fragile.

It exists in the founder's head, in the memory of one or two longest-tenured advisors, and nowhere else.

Atrium's first job, on every family install, is to extract and version that reasoning before it is lost.

This is not an interview. It is not a memoir. It is structured elicitation, done by an operator who knows what the schema can hold, in working sessions that may run weekly for the first six weeks of an engagement. The reasoning is captured as wiki pages — one decision at a time, one entity at a time — with citations into the underlying documents the founder is referring to as he speaks.

The pages are then read back to him. Not in the next session. The next morning. The founder approves the framings, sharpens the language, corrects the misreads. The pages are signed off and committed. Now they exist outside his head.

This is the work that the heirs will quietly thank Atrium for, twenty years later, in a moment no one will be able to schedule in advance.

The founder's reasoning is the most underestimated argument for starting an Atrium engagement *while the founder is still in the room*. The compounding case requires that he be present for the install. Once he is gone, the recovery cost is unbounded.
